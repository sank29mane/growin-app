# 28-01-PLAN: Institutional Liquidity & Slippage Modeling

## Objective
Implement institutional-grade liquidity analysis and slippage estimation to prevent high-impact orders from eroding profits on large-cap or low-float trades.

## Step 1: Liquidity & Slippage Model
- **Target**: `backend/quant_engine.py`
- **Action**: 
    - Implement a `calculate_slippage_estimate` method using a Square-Root Impact Model:
        - `Slippage (bps) = σ * Y * sqrt(Size / ADV)`
        - Where `σ` is daily volatility, `Y` is an impact coefficient (default 0.1), and `Size / ADV` is the order fraction.
    - Add a `max_participation_rate` (POV) check. Flag any order exceeding 10% of ADV.

## Step 2: MarketContext Expansion
- **Target**: `backend/market_context.py`
- **Action**: 
    - Add `slippage_bps: Optional[Decimal]` and `pov_participation: Optional[Decimal]` to `RiskGovernanceData`.
    - Add `liquidity_status: str` (e.g., "LIQUID", "THIN", "ILLIQUID").

## Step 3: DataFabricator Integration
- **Target**: `backend/data_fabricator.py`
- **Action**: 
    - Update `_fetch_macro_indicators` or create `_fetch_liquidity_metrics` to calculate slippage based on a standard "unit size" (e.g., £1,000 or 1% of portfolio).
    - Inject these metrics into the `RiskGovernanceData` object.

## Step 4: RiskAgent & DecisionAgent Enforcement
- **Target**: `backend/agents/risk_agent.py`, `backend/agents/decision_agent.py`
- **Action**:
    - **RiskAgent**: Block trades where estimated slippage > 1% of position value.
    - **DecisionAgent**: Adjust "Target Entry" to include slippage (e.g., Entry = Last + Slippage).

## Step 5: Verification
- Create `tests/backend/test_liquidity_deep_dive.py`.
- Verify that a large order on a low-ADV ticker (e.g., a small cap) triggers a "LIQUIDITY_BLOCKED" or "HIGH_SLIPPAGE" warning.
- Ensure the square-root model produces realistic bps estimates for M4 Pro/Max local inference.
